###Different Types of Cyber Attackers
Cyber attackers are individuals or groups who attempt to gain unauthorized access, steal data, or disrupt systems. They are classified based on skills, motivation, and intent.

---

###1 Black Hat Hackers.

Malicious attackers who break systems for personal gain.

####Motivation

- Money

- Data theft

- Ransom

####Examples

- Stealing bank credentials

- Launching ransomware attacks

---

###2 White Hat Hackers (Ethical Hackers).

Authorized security professionals who find vulnerabilities legally.

####Motivation

- Improve security

- Prevent cyber attacks

####Examples

- Penetration testers

- Bug bounty hunters

---

###3 Grey Hat Hackers

Act between white and black hats.

####Motivation

- Curiosity

- Fame

- Sometimes money

####Examples

- Finding vulnerabilities without permission and reporting them later

---

###4 Script Kiddies.

Unskilled attackers using ready-made tools.

####Motivation

- Fun

- Fame

- Learning

####Examples

- Using tools like LOIC

- Website defacement for fun

---

###5 Hacktivists.

Attack systems for political or social reasons.

####Motivation

- Ideology

- Protest

####Examples

- Website defacement

- DDoS against organizations

---

###6 Insider Attackers.

People inside the organization with legitimate access.

####Motivation

- Revenge

- Financial gain

- Negligence

####Examples

- Employee leaking customer data

- Misusing admin privileges

---

###7 Cyber Criminals.

Organized groups running cybercrime like a business.

####Motivation

- Profit

####Examples

- Online fraud

- Phishing campaigns

- Credit card theft

---

###8 Nation-State Attackers.

Government-sponsored attackers.

####Motivation

- Espionage

- Cyber warfare

####Examples

- Attacking power grids

- Stealing military or political data

---

###9 Botnet Operators.

Control networks of infected devices.

####Motivation

- DDoS attacks

- Spam distribution

####Examples

- Using IoT devices to flood servers

---
